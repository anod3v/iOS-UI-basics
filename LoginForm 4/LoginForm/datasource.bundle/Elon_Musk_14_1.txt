In 1995, Musk and his brother, Kimbal, started Zip2, a web software company, with money raised from a small group of angel investors.[44] The company developed and marketed an internet city guide for the newspaper publishing industry, with maps, directions and yellow pages,[64] with the vector graphics mapping and direction code being implemented by Musk in Java.[65][better source needed] Musk obtained contracts with The New York Times and the Chicago Tribune,[66][67] and persuaded the board of directors to abandon plans for a merger with CitySearch.[68] Musk's attempts to become CEO were thwarted by the board.[47] Compaq acquired Zip2 for US$307 million in cash[47]:109 in February 1999.[69] Musk received US$22 million for his 7 percent share from the sale.[66][47]:109[67]
